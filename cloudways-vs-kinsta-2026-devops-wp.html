Cloudways vs Kinsta (2026): Which for DevOps vs WordPress?

Managed hosting comparison for full-stack and WP-heavy teams.

Cloudways and Kinsta both offer managed hosting but skew toward different use cases. For DevOps and full-stack (custom apps, Web3 nodes, CI/CD): Cloudways’ flexible VPS layer (DO/AWS/GCP) and root-level control usually win. For WordPress-heavy teams (agencies, content sites, WooCommerce): Kinsta’s WP-optimized stack, staging, and support are hard to beat. Cloudways: best for DevOps and full-stack

Cloudways gives you managed servers on DigitalOcean, AWS, or Google Cloud with SSH, cron, and stack control. Ideal for custom apps, Node/PHP stacks, Web3 or RPC nodes, and teams that want “managed” without losing flexibility. You get dedicated resources, advanced caching (e.g. Object Cache Pro), and optional Cloudflare add-on. If your workload isn’t mostly WordPress, or you need multi-app and custom pipelines, Cloudways is the stronger fit.

Kinsta: best for WordPress-first

Kinsta is built for WordPress: optimized PHP, automatic updates, staging, and WP-specific support. Great for agencies running many WP sites, high-traffic content or WooCommerce, and teams that want “WordPress done for you” with minimal ops. You trade some infrastructure flexibility for WP expertise and a polished dashboard. If WordPress is your core workload, Kinsta is the stronger fit.
