What a FinOps Audit Actually Covers

A FinOps audit is an independent review of your cloud infrastructure spend — not a vendor pitch. It maps where your budget is going across compute, storage, data transfer, and licensing, then identifies where you are over-provisioned, under-utilized, or paying list price when committed or reserved pricing would cut the bill by 30–40%.

Most mid-market teams running on AWS, GCP, or Azure are paying for capacity they provisioned 12–18 months ago and never right-sized. That is normal. What a proper audit does is turn those stale configurations into a concrete action list — sorted by dollar impact, not engineering effort.

The 3 Biggest Cloud Cost Mistakes in 2026

  • Over-provisioned compute that was never revisited. Instances sized for launch-day traffic rarely get downsized after load stabilizes. Our AWS rightsizing guide shows exactly which EC2 and RDS families move the bill most.
  • Egress charges treated as fixed costs. Data transfer out fees look small per GB but compound fast at scale. Moving traffic through a CDN or WAF can cut egress 60–80%. See how to reduce egress costs with a CDN and WAF.
  • No structured optimization checklist across the full stack. Storage, snapshots, idle load balancers, and zombie resources rarely get reviewed in the same pass. Our cloud cost optimization checklist covers all of them in a single repeatable process.
