2. Noisy Neighbors vs. Bare Metal Performance

Solana node runners and latency-sensitive Node.js applications require consistent p99 latency. Hyperscaler VMs often suffer from "CPU Steal" when other tenants on the same physical host spike. In the Firedancer era, even a 50ms jitter can cause your node to drift or miss blocks.

Vultr's advantage: Access to Bare Metal instances with dedicated NVMe storage. You get the full physical resource without a hypervisor tax. On AWS, a comparable bare metal instance often costs 3x more and comes with complex VPC networking overhead.

Orchestrator Tip: If you are running an RPC node for Solana or a high-frequency trading bot, your bottleneck isn't raw CPU—it's Deterministic Jitter. Bare metal is the only way to win in 2026.

3. When to Choose Vultr vs. AWS
