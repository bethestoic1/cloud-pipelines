What is SiteGround?

SiteGround is a managed WordPress and web hosting provider founded in 2004 and now running on Google Cloud infrastructure. It offers shared hosting, cloud hosting, and managed WordPress — all with a heavy focus on ease of use for non-technical site owners. SiteGround is consistently rated among the top three WordPress hosts by ease of use, support quality, and out-of-the-box performance.

For a first WordPress site, SiteGround bundles nearly everything you need: CDN, SSL, email, daily backups, WP auto-updates, and a drag-and-drop site builder — starting at $2.99/mo introductory. The catch is the renewal rate.

SiteGround pricing 2026

Budget for $17–36/mo from year two, and SiteGround's value proposition still holds for many site owners. Performance

SiteGround runs on Google Cloud with their proprietary SuperCacher technology (three layers of caching: static, dynamic, and memcached). Combined with the free Cloudflare CDN, SiteGround delivers fast page loads for typical WordPress sites without any configuration. Core Web Vitals scores are generally good out of the box.

For very high-traffic sites or WooCommerce with many concurrent sessions, SiteGround shared hosting starts to hit resource limits. Their cloud hosting plans scale better, but at that point Cloudways on Google Cloud offers more control for similar money.

Support quality

SiteGround's support is a genuine strength. All plans include 24/7 live chat, and GoGeek includes phone support. Response times are typically under 3 minutes, and agents are generally competent on WordPress-specific issues. The knowledge base is extensive and well-maintained. For non-technical site owners, this support quality is a meaningful differentiator.

Ease of use

SiteGround uses a custom control panel (Site Tools) that is cleaner than cPanel and well-optimized for WordPress. Setting up a new site, installing WordPress, connecting a domain, and configuring email takes under 10 minutes. The SG Optimizer plugin configures caching and performance settings without requiring technical knowledge. SiteGround is one of the easiest managed hosts to get started with.

Who should use SiteGround?

SiteGround is the right pick for: bloggers and content sites launching their first WordPress site on a budget, small businesses that need email hosting bundled with hosting, non-technical site owners who want strong support and easy setup, and anyone who wants a proven managed WordPress host for year one without a large upfront commitment.

Who should NOT use SiteGround?

If you are running more than one or two WordPress sites, Cloudways at $11–24/mo offers unlimited sites with no renewal hike — SiteGround's GrowBig plan renews at $29.99/mo for unlimited sites but with lower resources. For agencies, SiteGround doesn't scale economically. If you need staging on every site, GrowBig or GoGeek is required (not StartUp). If your site grows past 100,000 visits/mo, you will need to upgrade plans or move to a more scalable solution.

Verdict: SiteGround is excellent for year one — budget for the renewal rate

At $2.99/mo intro with CDN, SSL, email, and support included, SiteGround is one of the best value entry-level WordPress hosts available. The catch is real: year two costs $17.99–35.99/mo. FAQ

Is SiteGround good for WordPress in 2026?

Yes — SiteGround is a well-regarded managed WordPress host with good performance, reliable uptime, and strong support. It is particularly well-suited for first-time WordPress site owners and small businesses. The main drawback is the renewal pricing model, which increases significantly after the introductory period.

What is SiteGround's renewal price?

SiteGround's introductory prices are for the first billing term only. At renewal: StartUp goes from $2.99/mo to approximately $17.99/mo; GrowBig from $4.99/mo to approximately $29.99/mo; GoGeek from $7.99/mo to approximately $35.99/mo. Prices are for 12-month billing and vary by promotion and region.

Does SiteGround include email hosting?

Yes — SiteGround includes free email hosting on all plans. This is a meaningful advantage over Cloudways and Kinsta, which do not include email and require Google Workspace or Zoho as an add-on.

Does SiteGround offer a free trial?

SiteGround does not offer a free trial but provides a 30-day money-back guarantee on new hosting purchases.

How does SiteGround compare to Cloudways?

SiteGround is easier to set up and includes email, CDN, and SSL out of the box. Cloudways gives more control, unlimited sites per server, and no renewal price hikes starting at $11/mo. For a single site or beginner, SiteGround's intro pricing wins year one. For agencies or multi-site setups, Cloudways wins on long-term value. See our full Cloudways vs SiteGround comparison.

Is SiteGround worth the price after renewal?

At $17.99/mo for one site (StartUp renewal), SiteGround is competitive with Cloudways ($11/mo for unlimited sites). For a single site where you want email, CDN, and phone support bundled, it is reasonable value. For multiple sites or agencies, Cloudways offers significantly better economics.

Can I migrate to SiteGround from another host?

Yes. SiteGround provides one free migration on GrowBig and GoGeek plans. They also offer a WordPress Migrator plugin for self-managed migrations from any host. StartUp plan migrations are paid.
