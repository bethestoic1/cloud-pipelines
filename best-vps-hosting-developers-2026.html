Why Cloudways leads for most developers

Cloudways sits on top of DigitalOcean, AWS, GCP, Vultr, and Hetzner — you get the underlying compute at a small markup in exchange for managed operations: automated backups, server monitoring, OS patching, and a clean dashboard. For a solo developer or agency, the $10–20/mo management premium is worth it versus spending hours on server maintenance.

When to go with a raw VPS (Hostinger, DigitalOcean, Vultr)

Self-managed VPS makes sense when you're comfortable with the CLI and want maximum control. Hostinger is the best starting point for solo devs at $4.99/mo. For teams needing Kubernetes, managed databases, or object storage, DigitalOcean's ecosystem (Spaces, DOKS, Managed Postgres) and Vultr's global footprint both justify the higher price. Budget-conscious EU teams may also consider Hetzner for raw price-performance, though Hetzner has fewer regions and no managed Kubernetes.

FAQ

Is Cloudways worth the premium over raw VPS?

For most developers and agencies, yes. Cloudways adds automated backups, OS patching, server monitoring, staging environments, and a GUI — saving hours of setup and maintenance. The markup is roughly $8–15/mo over a raw Droplet, which is cheap compared to DevOps time.

Is Hostinger VPS good for developers?

Hostinger VPS is a solid entry point at $4.99/mo — full root access, your choice of Ubuntu/Debian/CentOS, and SSH from day one. It lacks managed services (no managed Postgres, no K8s), but for solo projects, side apps, or learning the command line, it's hard to beat at this price. Upgrade to Cloudways when you outgrow it.

Which VPS has the best free trial?

DigitalOcean offers $200 in credit over 60 days. Vultr offers $250 over 30 days. Cloudways offers a 3-day trial with no credit card. Hostinger offers a 30-day money-back guarantee. For evaluating managed hosting, Cloudways' trial is the simplest.

Can I use Cloudways with other cloud providers?

Yes. Cloudways supports DigitalOcean, Vultr, AWS, GCP, and Hetzner as underlying cloud providers, giving you their compute at your chosen price-performance level with Cloudways' managed layer on top.

What about Hetzner for budget hosting?

Hetzner offers excellent price-performance in Europe (CX22 at ~€4.35/mo), but has only 5 data center regions (EU + US), no managed Kubernetes, and no affiliate program. For most developers, Hostinger offers a comparable price with more global reach and easier onboarding.
